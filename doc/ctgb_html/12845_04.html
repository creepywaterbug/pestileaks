2 DETAILS VAN HET VERZOEK EN DE TOELATING


2.1 Verzoek

Het betreft een verzoek tot wijziging van de toelating van het middel Bellis (12845 N), een middel op basis van de werkzame stoffen pyraclostrobin en boscalid. Het middel is uitsluitend toegelaten als schimmelbestrijdingsmiddel in de teelt van appel en peer.


De gevraagde wijzigingen betreffen:

Verruiming van de driftreducerende maatregelen.


2.2 Informatie met betrekking tot de stof


Boscalid is een nieuwe stof waarvan een DAR beschikbaar is. Duitsland is RMS.


Pyraclostrobin is een nieuwe stof die is geplaatst op bijlage I van richtlijn 91/414/EEG sinds
1 juni 2004.


2.3 Karakterisering van het middel

Bellis is een preventief werkend middel op basis van pyraclostrobin en boscalid.

Boscalid behoort tot de chemische groep van de carboxamiden. Enkele werkzame stoffen binnen deze groep zijn o.a. flutolanil, mepronil, carboxin, fenfuram en thifluzamide. Boscalid remt de ademhaling in de mitochondria in de schimmel, waardoor essentiรซle bouwstenen niet meer worden gevormd (succinate dehydrogenase) en de schimmelgroei wordt geremd. Het middel remt tevens de kieming van de schimmelsporen. Boscalid werkt preventief en systemisch en heeft een grote translaminaire mobiliteit. Uit veldonderzoek is gebleken dat boscalid een zeer hoge effectiviteit heeft tegen een breed scala van schimmels. Boscalid is een โsingle site inhibitorโโ en is matig resistentiegevoelig.

Pyraclostrobin behoort tot de groep van de strobilurinen. Het Fungicide Resistance Action Committee (FRAC) geeft aan dat strobilurinen in hoge mate resistentiegevoelig zijn. Enkele werkzame stoffen binnen deze groep zijn kresoxim-methyl, azoxystrobin, famoxadone en trifloxystrobin. Pyraclostrobin remt de mitochondrische ademhaling. Het middel remt de kieming van schimmelsporen en de groei van de kiembuis op het bladoppervlak. Pyraclostrobin is niet systemisch.

Drinking water criterion

It follows from the decision of the Court of Appeal on Trade and Industry of 19 August 2005 (Awb 04/37 (General Administrative Law Act)) that when considering an application, the Ctgb should, on the basis of the scientific and technical knowledge and taking into account the data submitted with the application, also judge the application according to the drinking water criterion โsurface water intended for drinking water productionโ. No mathematical model for this aspect is available. This means that any data that is available cannot be adequately taken into account. It is therefore not possible to arrive at a scientifically well-founded assessment according to this criterion. The Ctgb has not been given the instruments for testing surface water from which drinking water is produced according to the drinking water criterion. In order to comply with the Courtโs decision, however - from which it can be concluded that the Ctgb should make an effort to give an opinion on this point โ and as provisional measure, to avoid a situation where no authorisation at all can be granted during the development of a model generation of the data necessary, the Ctgb has investigated whether the product under consideration and the active substance could give cause for concern about the drinking water criterion.


As both pyraclostrobin and boscalid are new active substances, there are no data available regarding its presence in surface water at drinking water abstraction points. At the moment, a preliminary decision tree is used to address this matter, while waiting for the final decision tree that is currently under construction by the Drinking Water Criterion Project Team. The following procedure is derived from this preliminary decision tree: Because exceeding of the threshold value of 0.1 ยตg/L at drinking water abstraction points in surface water cannot be entirely ruled out for pyraclostrobin or boscalid based on the predicted exposure concentrations in the ditch next to the arable land (as calculated with TOXSWA), an adequate risk assessment is required to ensure that the risk of exceeding the drinking water criterion at drinking water abstraction points in surface water is negligible. The Ctgb has established a provisional and conservative procedure to enable expert judgement on this matter in anticipation of the final decision tree. In this procedure, a dilution factor of 10 and a residence time of 14 days are used to estimate surface water concentrations at the drinking water abstraction points. When expected concentrations are between 0.1 and 0.5 ยตg/L, post registration monitoring is required at the so-called blue junctions (as established in the Water Framework Directive). These are situated upstream from drinking water abstraction points. Thus, when the surface water concentrations at these locations are below 0.1 ยตg/L, it can be considered sufficiently certain that there will be no exceeding of the drinking water threshold at the drinking water abstraction points.


Pyraclostrobin

For pyraclostrobin, this procedure results in an worst-case estimated concentration of 1.08 ยตg/l divided by the dilution factor of 10= 0.108 ยตg/L. Taking into account the residence period of 14 days and the mean degradation half-life in water/sediment systems of 27.9 days will result in a further decrease of this concentration, yielding a final estimated concentration of 0.0763 ยตg/L. BF 500-11, BF 500-13 and BF 500-14 are expected to be lower than pyraclostrobin.

Therefore, for pyraclostrobin and its metabolites, it is not expected to exceed the drinking water criterion. The standards for surface water destined for the production of drinking water are met.


Boscalid

As boscalid is a new active substance, no knowledge exists on its potential presence in surface water. At the moment, a preliminary decision tree is used to address this matter, while waiting for the final decision tree that is currently under construction by the Drinking Water Criterion Project Team. The following procedure is derived from this preliminary decision tree. Because exceeding of the threshold value of 0.1 ยตg/L at drinking water abstraction points in surface water cannot be entirely ruled out for boscalid based on the predicted exposure concentrations in the ditch next to the arable land (as calculated with TOXSWA), an adequate risk assessment is required to ensure that the risk of exceeding the drinking water criterion at drinking water abstraction points in surface water is negligible.

An adequate risk assessment for boscalid was provided by the applicant on January 3 2006 (Boscalid and the Dutch drinking water criterion - Risk analysis according the preliminary Dutch decision tree).

The CTB model of November 2005 assumes that the surface water concentration in the ditch (edge of field) is decisive for the concentration at the drinking water abstraction points. This approach does not take into account the dilution factor and the travelling time of the water from the field ditch to the abstraction point. In a meeting of the drinking water criterion working group installed by the ministries of agricultural and environment to draft a decision tree, a dilution factor of 10 and a travelling time of 6 days was proposed. Newer discussions in this working group brought in the Relative Area Treated (RAT) or a RAT factor as an alternative for the dilution factor of 10. Both approaches were argued for with the fact that the plant protection product will be applied only to a fraction of the area close to the ditch because

- only a share of the area will be of agricultural land use,

- regarding the agricultural area close to a ditch, the crop protection product is or will not be registered on all crops,

- in the crops with registered use the product is under competition with other products and the field will only be partly sprayed with the product under review.


Following latest discussion, therefore the present assessment takes into account the estimated relative treated area for boscalid rather than an over-all dilution factor.

The share of agricultural land use is taken from landuse census data of the Netherlands (CBS 2004). The same data source provides information of the share of agricultural land used for the different crops in which boscalid is intended to be used.

Furthermore, in this paper a market share of 40% for boscalid containing products will be used in the calculations. This % is high, overestimating the future use. Especially in large crops, which contribute relatively high to the loading of surface water, heavy competition is present. In the case of boscalid a resistance strategy is in place, which prevents often to reach even this 40% market share.

The assumptions in the adequate risk assessment are reasonable and therefore agreed upon.

Combination toxicology

Combination toxicology is assessed for formulations containing more than one active substance, and for combinations of products, which are made according to the Instructions for Use as a tank mixture. Based on the precautionary principle, concentration-addition is assumed.

For pesticides the TER (Toxicity-Exposure Ratio) is used as a standard in the risk assessment (except for bees and other non-target arthropods, where HQ-values are calculated). The TER must be higher than a trigger value to comply with the standards.

For the combination risk assessment of formulations containing more than one active substance and for tank mixtures the following formula is used:

triggersubstance 1 /TERsubstance 1 + triggersubstance 2 /TERsubstance 2 + triggersubstance i/TERsubstance i .


When for each substance the trigger values are equal, the combined TER value can be calculated according to:

TERcombi = trigger/((trigger/TERsubstance 1)+(trigger/TERsubstance 2)+( trigger/TERsubstance 3))


An acceptable risk is expected when TERcombi > trigger.


In this formula, โtriggersโ are the trigger values as mentioned in the corresponding chapter of the HTB (v1.0).

In case toxicity of the formulation has been measured, the TER-value of the formulation is calculated with the PEC of the formulation and the toxicity value of the formulation. The PEC of the formulation is the sum of the PECs of the individual active substances. The toxicity value of the formulation is expressed in total amount active substance. Trigger/TER must be smaller than 1.

In the risk assessment, the risk of combination toxicology is assessed using the highest trigger/TER-value from the one based on the sum of the individual substances and the one based on formulation studies. When the standard of 1 is breached, the product is not permissible, unless an adequate risk assessment shows that there are no unacceptable effects under field conditions after application of the product according to the proposed GAP.
